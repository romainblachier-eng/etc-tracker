Stopping GLP-1 medications like Mounjaro can trigger rapid weight regain in many patients. These drugs work by regulating appetite and fullness through the nervous system. Once discontinued, these mechanisms gradually normalize, typically leading to increased hunger and food cravings.

Research indicates that without sustained lifestyle changes, patients usually recover 50 to 80% of lost weight within a year after stopping treatment. This regain is not inevitable—it largely depends on adopting new eating habits and maintaining physical activity established during therapy.

Mounjaro functions as a temporary tool rather than a permanent solution. Its effectiveness relies on a comprehensive approach combining appropriate nutrition and regular exercise. Physicians recommend consolidating these changes before considering discontinuation.

Some patients continue treatment indefinitely under medical supervision, while others successfully maintain results after stopping thanks to established habits. Success hinges on proper preparation: gradual dose reduction coupled with intensified behavioral efforts before complete discontinuation. The transition period is critical, as it allows the body to adjust while reinforcing the lifestyle modifications that support long-term weight management.
