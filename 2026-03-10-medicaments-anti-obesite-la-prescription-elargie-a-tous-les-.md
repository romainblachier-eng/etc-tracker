France is expanding access to breakthrough obesity treatments, marking a significant shift in healthcare strategy. Previously, only endocrinologists and obesity specialists could prescribe GLP-1 medications like Mounjaro (tirzeppatide). This restriction severely limited patient access. Now, general practitioners and other medical specialists are authorized to prescribe these innovative drugs. Tirzeppatide works by regulating appetite and improving weight control by targeting two natural hormones in the body. This expansion comes as obesity affects approximately 17% of the French adult population, making it a pressing public health concern. The measure aims to increase treatment accessibility and reduce waiting times at specialist offices. However, general practitioners will receive clear guidelines to ensure appropriate use and thorough patient monitoring. This decision recognizes these medications as essential therapeutic tools rather than lifestyle alternatives alone. It represents part of a broader national strategy to improve obesity management in France. The policy change reflects evolving medical understanding of obesity as a chronic condition requiring professional intervention and ongoing support.
