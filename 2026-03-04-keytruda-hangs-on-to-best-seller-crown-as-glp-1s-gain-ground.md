Despite the spectacular rise of GLP-1 medications, Keytruda immunotherapy maintains its position as the world's best-selling drug. This cancer treatment, based on immune checkpoint inhibitors, remains the pharmaceutical industry's top revenue generator, even as GLP-1s like tirzepatide (Mounjaro) and semaglutide gain significant market share. Originally developed for diabetes management, these GLP-1 drugs are now widely prescribed for weight management, expanding their reach beyond their initial indication. The growth trajectory of GLP-1s is remarkable, yet Keytruda's commercial dominance persists through its versatility across multiple cancer types and ongoing patent protection. Keytruda works by reactivating the patient's immune system to fight melanoma, lung cancer, and other malignancies. While GLP-1 medications are reshaping the therapeutic landscape with expanding applications beyond endocrinology, they currently operate in different market segments than traditional oncology treatments. This coexistence of pharmaceutical blockbusters reflects the evolving healthcare market, where multiple therapies can thrive simultaneously by addressing distinct medical needs and patient populations.
