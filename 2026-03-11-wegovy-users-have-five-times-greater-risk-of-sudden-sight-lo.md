A recent study raises important questions about the eye safety of GLP-1 medications. Researchers found that Wegovy (semaglutide) users had five times higher risk of sudden age-related macular degeneration compared to Ozempic (semaglutide at different dosage) users. This condition affects central vision and can lead to sudden, severe sight loss.

Macular degeneration impacts the macula, the retina's most sensitive area responsible for detailed vision. Although both medications contain the same active ingredient, dosages vary significantly: Wegovy uses higher doses for weight loss management, while Ozempic uses lower concentrations for diabetes treatment.

This finding doesn't mean all Wegovy users will experience vision problems, but it warrants closer medical attention. Healthcare providers recommend that patients on these medications undergo regular eye examinations and promptly report any vision changes. The discovery underscores the importance of ongoing research into long-term effects of these increasingly popular treatments, particularly for people with diabetes or obesity. Patients should not stop treatment without consulting their healthcare team, but should remain vigilant about eye health monitoring while using these medications.
