Eli Lilly has identified an impurity in certain batches of tirzepatide compounded at pharmacies. Mounjaro, the blockbuster medication for diabetes and weight loss, is normally manufactured by the American company under strict quality control. However, "compounded" versions—prepared by pharmacies from raw ingredients—can have manufacturing defects. This discovery raises serious questions about the safety of counterfeit or poorly prepared drugs. The impurity found could affect treatment effectiveness and safety. Eli Lilly is collaborating with health authorities to investigate the scope of the problem and inform healthcare professionals. Tirzepatide belongs to GLP-1 receptor agonists, a revolutionary drug class that acts on appetite and sugar metabolism. Facing strong global demand and periodic shortages, some patients turn to cheaper compounded versions. This incident underscores that pharmaceutical quality is far from trivial: every manufacturing step matters to ensure reliable treatment. Health authorities recommend patients consult their doctor before switching supply sources. The incident highlights ongoing tensions between drug accessibility, cost, and safety in healthcare.
